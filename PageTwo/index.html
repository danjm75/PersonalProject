Daniel J Mendoza

My first involvement with programming started August 1st of 2015. My brother-in-law sent me an article about Galvanize and the value being seen in bootcamps. I was very interested by the end of the article and started looking into the many bootcamps and what they each had to offer. During my preparation for Galvanize I used as many tutorials as I had time for. Codecademy was my first which was advised on the Galvanize website. I am including links to some of the tutorials and resources I used. I am sure I am leaving some out. Between YouTube, (my) Github, Codewars, Stack Overflow, Meetup and all the other resources, I have been kept very busy. The Meetups and the forums have been a great place to get restarted when I feel like I am stuck and should drive my truck over my laptop! It's grounding to hear others going through the same frustrations as I am.

This is a interesting article I found and would like to follow up on. This is one of the reasons I like JavaScript and programming. The options seem to be limitless, and only bound by our imaginations and our interests. I am very excited to be a part of this technology and to see where this road takes me.
Articles like this one about Jack Dorsey are nice to read when I am feeling frustrated and ready to quit! His story to me, and so many other stories of people like you and I that, through hard work and sweat and maybe a few tears, reach that level of expertise in their field that they worked so hard for. Whether I become a "billionaire" or not, I want to be known for my quality.
